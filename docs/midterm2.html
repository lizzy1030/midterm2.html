Ariana Grande

Career

  • 2013: Debut album
    In 2013, She released her debut album, 'Yours Truly', which she described as a "throwback" to the R&B music of the 1990s. The first single released from her album was 'The Way' and featured her former boyfriend Mac Miller.
  • 2014: 'Problem'
    It wasn't until 2014 and the single 'Problem' that Ariana Grande saw chart success in the UK. The music video for the song, won Best Pop Video at the 2014 MTV Video Music Awards and has now been viewed more than 1 billion times on YouTube.
  • 2016: 'Dangerous Woman'
    Ariana also embarked on her biggest tour to date - the 'Dangerous Woman Tour' supporting her third album 'Dangerous Woman'. Devastatingly, her show at Manchester Arena on 22nd May 2017 ended with a horrific tragedy when a bomb killed 22 people and injured many others.
  • 2018: 'Sweetener'
    Ariana in August 2018 with her 'Sweetener' album which peaked at Number 1 in the UK and US, also in many other countries around the world. It catalogued her dealing with everything she'd been through, as well as her relationship with Pete Davidson, which ended in October 2018.
  • 2018: 'thank u, next'
    Ariana suffered yet more loss in 2018 when her ex-boyfriend and close friend Mac Miller passed away. She paid tribute to him and even referenced him as an "angel" in her song 'thank u, next'.
  • 2020: Surprise release of her sixth album
    Ariana announced in October 2020 that her sixth album would be released,that month! At the time, she released no other information about the record, keep her fans in total suspense.
  • 2021: Ariana, Doja Cat and Megan Thee Stallion
    2021, Ariana released a remix of her track '34+35' that featured hugenames Doja Cat and Megan Thee Stallion and was a massive hit with fans. Later that year, Ariana returned the favour to Doja Cat, featuring on 'I Don't Do Drugs' from Doja's third album 'Planet Her'.
